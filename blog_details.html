WhatsApp starts rolling out a feature to send messages to yourself

he instant messaging service WhatsApp has started rolling out a new feature that allows you to send messages to yourself.

It is reported that WABetaInfo - which specializes in monitoring experimental WhatsApp features - estimated this feature for the first time in the beta version (2.22.24.11) of the WhatsApp application months ago, but since this feature was not compatible with multiple devices, the WhatsApp service abandoned it for a while, Yesterday, however, the site monitored its presence in the final version (22.23.74) of the application for iOS users. Why do you need to send messages to yourself in WhatsApp? Many people find it helpful to take notes, or save URLs in a message for themselves; They can easily come back to it later, a feature that some popular messaging apps offer; Such as: Telegram, and Signal for a long time, as well as work applications such as: (Slack) Slack. You will find this feature in the Telegram application under the name (Saved Messages), and the application also allows you to create a private channel that you can send messages to yourself through as well. How do you send a message to yourself in WhatsApp? Simply open a new conversation, and when you select a contact to message, your personal contact will appear at the top of your contact list. In addition, when you open it, a different caption will appear for the chat, where you will find the phrase (Message yourself) under the name of your contact as it appears in the screenshot.

Messages sent to yourself will be visible across all of your connected devices, so WhatsApp also maintains end-to-end encryption in these chats. If you don't find this new feature in the app, you may have to wait a few more days for WhatsApp to complete the rollout. It is noteworthy that the WhatsApp service has recently launched a feature that allows users to re-listen to their voice messages before sending them. send it. WhatsApp is also currently working on a new feature called Companion Mode, which allows you to use one account in more than one phone, as it was spotted by (WABetaInfo) yesterday in the latest beta version of the application available in the Google Play Store.
